Apple Crisp

A Slice of Warm Apple Crisp with a Scoop of Ice Cream

This simple, delicious dessert consists of baked chopped apples along with a streusel topping. It is synonymous with fall and comfort and is one of the easiest baked desserts to prepare.

Apple Crisp is relatively modern in terms of food, having first been recorded in 1924. It became more popular during WWII when access to ingredients for apple pies was limited by rationing. It is especially popular in the United States, Canada, and England.

Ingredients

  • 6 Granny Smith apples, peeled and finely sliced (other varieties of apple can be used
  • 2 Tbsp Granulated Sugar
  • 1 3/4 tsp Ground Cinnammon
  • 1 1/2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 Cup Light Brown Sugar
  • 3/4 Cup Old Fashioned Oats
  • 3/4 Cup All-Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 Cup Cold Unsalted Butter, diced into small cubes
  • Pinch of Kosher Salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 F degrees. Butter an 8x8 baking dish. Set aside.
  2. Combine apples, granulated sugar, 3/4 tsp cinnamon, and lemon juice. Transfer to baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine brown sugar, oats, flour, 1 tsp cinnamon, salt, butter. Use a pastry cutter (or fork) to cut the butter into the oat mixture until it resembles pea-sized crumbs
  4. Spread topping over the apples in the baking dish and gently pat down evenly. Bake 40-50 minutes or until golden brown and bubbly.
  5. Best Served warm with vanilla iced cream or whipped cream. Enjoy!
