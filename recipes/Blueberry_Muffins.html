Blueberry Muffins

Freshly Baked Blueberry Muffins

Blueberry muffins date back to the 18th century. When English colonists discovered blueberries, which are native only to North America, they added them to baked goods and carried both the recipe and blueberries back to England.

Originally, European bakers used a berry known as "bilberry" for baking muffins, scones, pies, etc. but were unable to find it in America which led to them adopting the native blueberries.

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups Flour
  • 1/4 Cup Sugar
  • 1 Tbsp Baking Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1 1/2 Cups Plain Yogurt
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Stick Unsalted Butter
  • 1 Cup Blueberries

Steps

  1. Whisk dry ingredients together - flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt.
  2. Whisk yogurt and eggs together in a separate bowl.
  3. Fold wet ingredients into dry ingredients until just combined.
  4. Fold in melted butter and blueberries.
